Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Pace Consulting may collect, use, store, and protect personal data through this website and in connection with website enquiries. It is drafted to align with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Who we are

Pace Consulting is the controller of personal data collected through this website for the purposes described in this policy.

What personal data we may collect

We may collect and process personal data that you choose to provide to us, such as your name, email address, company details, and the contents of any enquiry you send to us. We may also collect limited technical information such as IP address, browser type, device information, and usage data through standard website analytics and server logs.

How we use personal data

  • to respond to your enquiries and communicate with you;
  • to operate, maintain, and improve the website;
  • to understand website performance and usage patterns;
  • to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims where necessary;
  • to comply with legal and regulatory obligations.

Lawful bases

Where UK GDPR applies, our lawful bases may include legitimate interests in operating and improving our website and business, taking steps at your request before entering into a contract, compliance with legal obligations, and, where required, your consent.

Cookies and analytics

This website may use standard analytics and similar technologies to measure performance and understand usage. Where non-essential cookies are used, an appropriate cookie mechanism should be implemented so that consent can be obtained where required by law.

Sharing personal data

We do not sell personal data. We may share data with trusted service providers who support website hosting, analytics, communications, IT, or professional advisory services, but only where appropriate safeguards and contractual protections are in place. We may also disclose data where required by law or to protect legal rights.

International transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the UK, we will take reasonable steps to ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, such as adequacy regulations or approved contractual mechanisms, where required.

Data retention

We retain personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes set out in this policy, including responding to enquiries, maintaining business records, complying with legal obligations, and resolving disputes.

Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you may have rights to request access to your personal data, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, objection to processing, data portability, and withdrawal of consent where consent is relied upon. You may also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office in the UK.

Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, but no online system can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

Contact

For privacy-related enquiries, please contact hello@paceconsulting.ai.

Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The latest version published on this website will apply.
